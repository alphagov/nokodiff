A goose incident is when a goose or geese displays threatening or aggressive behaviour which is clearly aimed towards a person or group of people.

Read guidance on what to do during a goose incident.

Call 999 if you or someone else is in immediate danger.

If you’ve been the victim or witness of a goose incident, you can:

  • report it online
  • claim compensation of up to £573, if you or your property have been damaged
  • get temporary accommodation, if you cannot stay in your home because the goose is occupying it
  • report someone for causing the incident by provoking the goose or gaggle of geese
  • claim £1337.69 from the Ministry of Goose Incidents

You could get a fine of up to £500 if you’re found guilty of causing a goose incident.

If a goose incident caused any damage to you or your property

You can claim compensation if the incident happened on or after 1 January 2024.

There are 3 levels of goose incidents. The amount you might get depends on the severity of the incident.

Mild goose incident

newline

This may involve a goose or gaggle of geese:

  • hissing at you
  • honking at you
  • following you to see what you’re doing
  • looking through your windows

Mild goose incidents do not usually involve any physical attacks or property damage. 

You cannot claim compensation for mild goose incidents.

Moderate goose incident

This may involve a goose or gaggle of geese:

  • chasing you to try and steal anything you’re carrying
  • causing minor property damage
  • taking things from your garden or home

Moderate goose incidents do not usually involve physical attacks.

You could get up to £250 compensation for moderate goose incidents.

Severe goose incident

This may involve a goose or gaggle of geese:

  • attacking you and causing harm
  • causing serious property damage 
  • refusing to leave your property

You could get up to £573 compensation for severe goose incidents.

If damages exceed £573

In some circumstances, you may be able to get more compensation. Contact your local council for more information

Get temporary accommodation 

Contact your local council for help getting temporary accommodation if you cannot live in your home because: 

  • it has been damaged
  • a goose or gaggle of geese have taken over your home

If you think someone is encouraging goose incidents

It’s illegal to deliberately encourage a goose incident by provoking or disturbing a goose or gaggle of geese. 

This can include: 

  • throwing objects at a goose
  • taunting a goose
  • following a goose if they try to move away
  • entering a goose’s territory

You can report someone encouraging goose incidents anonymously to Crimestoppers.
